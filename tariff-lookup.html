📖 What Tariffs Are Currently In Effect? (March 2026)
⚖️
SCOTUS Ruling — Feb 20, 2026: The Supreme Court ruled 6–3 in Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump that IEEPA does not authorize the President to impose tariffs. All IEEPA-based tariffs were immediately struck down. This included the "reciprocal" tariffs and the "fentanyl" China tariffs imposed in 2025.
🔴 STRUCK DOWN — IEEPA Tariffs (No longer in effect)
What they were: Tariffs imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA), including:
  • "Liberation Day" reciprocal tariffs (April 2025) — up to 145% on China, 46% Vietnam, etc.
  • China "fentanyl" tariffs (20% cumulative)
  • Country-specific reciprocal tariffs on all trading partners
Why struck down: IEEPA's "regulate" authority does not include raising tariff revenue; it's not the same as imposing import taxes.
🟡 NEW — Section 122 Global Surcharge (Currently in effect, temporary)
Rate: 15% ad valorem on all imports (started at 10%, raised to 15% on Feb 21, 2026)
Authority: Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 (balance of payments authority)
Effective: February 24, 2026
Expires: July 24, 2026 (hard 150-day statutory limit)
Extends to: Virtually all countries except USMCA/CAFTA qualifying goods
Key limit: Maximum 15% rate; requires an Act of Congress to extend beyond 150 days
🟣 STILL IN EFFECT — Section 301 China Tariffs
Origin: First imposed 2018–2019, originally under Trump 1.0, maintained by Biden, continued now
Authority: Section 301 of the Trade Act of 1974 (unfair trade practices)
Rates on apparel:
  • List 4A products (most finished apparel): 7.5%
  • Lists 1–3 products (fabrics, some accessories): 25%
Applies to: China only · Not affected by SCOTUS ruling · No expiration
🔵 ALWAYS IN EFFECT — MFN Base Tariff (Column 1)
What it is: The standard "Most Favored Nation" tariff rate set by Congress in the HTS schedule
Applies to: All WTO member countries
Rates on apparel: Typically 5%–32% depending on garment type and fabric
Example: Cotton knit shirt (6109.10.00) = 16.5% MFN
